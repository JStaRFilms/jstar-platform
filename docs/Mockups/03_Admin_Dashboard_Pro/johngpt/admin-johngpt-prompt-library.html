Here are 5 compelling YouTube title options for cooking content targeting home cooks:

1. "5 Pantry Staples That Make Home Cooking 10x Easier (No Chef Skills Needed)"
Why: Uses a specific number, addresses a pain point (complexity), and promises accessibility for beginners

2. "The 15-Minute Dinner Hack That Will Change How You Cook Forever"
Why: Creates curiosity with "hack," promises time savings, and uses strong language ("change forever")

3. "Why Your Pasta Sauce Always Falls Flat (And How to Fix It)"
Why: Identifies a common problem, creates curiosity, and promises a solution

4. "3 Cooking Mistakes You're Making Every Day (Number 2 Will Shock You)"
Why: Uses social proof ("every day"), creates curiosity with "shock," and includes a number

5. "The Lazy Chef's Guide to Gourmet Meals in 20 Minutes"
Why: Targets a specific identity ("lazy chef"), promises time efficiency, and sets expectations ("gourmet")

Testing Tip: Test your prompt with edge cases like "cooking" and "professional chefs" to ensure it handles different audience types appropriately.
